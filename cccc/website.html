About Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Early Life: Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (14 April 1891 – 6 December 1956) was born in Mhow (now Ambedkar Nagar), Madhya Pradesh. He received his education from the University of Mumbai, Columbia University (New York), Gray’s Inn (London), and London School of Economics.

Political Career: Dr. Ambedkar played a pivotal role in drafting the Constitution of India and served as the first Law and Justice Minister in the cabinet of Jawaharlal Nehru. He was associated with the Independent Labour Party, Scheduled Castes Federation, and Republican Party of India.

Social Reforms: He inspired the Dalit Buddhist movement after renouncing Hinduism, advocating for the rights of the oppressed and marginalized communities.

Legacy: Dr. Ambedkar, also known as Babasaheb, is hailed as the Father of the Indian Constitution and the Champion of the Dalits. His prominent achievements include chairing the Drafting Committee, serving as the First Law Minister of Independent India, and being a Labor Minister in the Viceroy’s Executive Council.
