Rani Lakshmi Bai

19 November 1828 - 18 June 1858

About Rani Lakshmi Bai

Early Life: Rani Lakshmi Bai, the Queen of Jhansi, was born on 19 November 1828 in Varanasi, India. She was named Manikarnika Tambe and was educated at home.

Role in the Rebellion: Lakshmi Bai became a symbol of resistance against British rule during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. She led her troops into battle and fought fiercely for her kingdom's independence.

Legacy: Rani Lakshmi Bai's courage and sacrifice are remembered as pivotal in India's struggle for independence. She remains an icon of bravery and patriotism.
